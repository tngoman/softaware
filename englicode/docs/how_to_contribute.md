The Concept:

To deprecate all vague, qualitative probability words (maybe, perhaps, definitely, rarely) and replace them with a strict, normalized probability scale based on Boolean logic.
The Rule & Syntax:

[Probability Value] 1

    The Logic: In probability theory, absolute impossibility is 0 and absolute certainty is 1. The anchor is always 1 (The Absolute Truth). The preceding number is the user's calculated decimal of certainty.

The Dictionary Replacements (The Leap):

    0 1 (Null / Impossible)
    Replaces: "No way," "Never," "Impossible."
    Context: "Is the feature shipping today?" "It's 0 1."
    The Leap: 0% probability. The event does not exist in the dataset.

    0.5 1 (The Coin Toss)
    Replaces: "Maybe," "50/50," "Could go either way."
    Context: "Are you coming to the meetup?" "I'm a 0.5 1 right now."
    The Leap: Perfect mathematical uncertainty. Equal chance of True or False.

    0.9 1 (High Confidence)
    Replaces: "Probably," "Almost definitely," "Most likely."
    Context: "Will the server hold the traffic?" "We are 0.9 1."
    The Leap: 90% probability. Approaching absolute truth, but acknowledging a 10% margin for anomaly.

    1 1 (Absolute Truth)
    Replaces: "Definitely," "100%," "Absolutely."
    Context: "Did you lock the database?" "1 1."
    The Leap: 100% probability. The event is a confirmed reality.

Developer Notes / Justification for Canon:

This protocol forces the speaker to quantify their confidence before speaking. If a client asks, "Will this be done by Friday?" answering with "Probably" is a logical cop-out. Answering with "0.7 1" forces the developer to admit there is a 30% chance of failure, allowing the client to manage risk accurately.

It seamlessly mirrors the existing Bandwidth Protocol ([Current] [Anchor]), but strictly limits the anchor to 1 to isolate it from capacity/resource discussions and firmly root it in probability math.
